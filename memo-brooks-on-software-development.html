Brooks on Software Development

Frederick Brooks in his classic paper, "No Silver Bullet - Essence and Accident in Software Engineering" - though pessimistic in title but realistic in content - has answered two prominent questions in software development.

Why software development is hard?

It is due to the nature of software itself. Brooks has duly observed that there are four essential software properties that contribute to this: complexity, conformity, changeability, and invinsibility.

What can we do about it?

Brooks has nailed it down by suggesting two keywords: prototype and organic. To develop the right software, use rapid prototyping to establish requirements. To develop the software right, grow software organically by adding more and more functions as they are run, tested and used.
