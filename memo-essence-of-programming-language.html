Essence of Programming Language

Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs, by Harold Abelson and Gerald Jay Sussman, has profoundly stated that the following three mechanisms are the essence of powerful programming language:

  • primitive elements, which represent the simplest elements in the language
  • means of combination, by which compound elements are built from simpler ones
  • means of abstraction, by which compound elements can be named and manipulated as units

Lisp (and its decendents) has realised this kind of power with simplicity and integrity by using only few primitive elements; list as the mean of combination; and first-class function as the mean of abstraction.
