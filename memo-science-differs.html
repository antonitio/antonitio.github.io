Science Differs

Yuval Harari in his book, Sapiens: A brief history of human kind, critically points out three ways in which science differs from previous human knowledge traditions:

  • The willingness to admit ignorance. It assumes we don't know everything, and accepts that the things which we think we know could be proven wrong as we gain more knowledge.
  • The centrality of observation and mathematics. With admitting ignorance, it aims to obtain new knowledge by gathering observations and using mathematical tools to connect them into comprehensive theories.
  • The acquisition of new powers. It applies these theories to acquire new power, particularly to develop new technologies.
